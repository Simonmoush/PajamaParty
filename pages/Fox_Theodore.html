POEM FOR MEGAN

i have climbed into the sky with you we are clutching at stars and shoving them under our scarves to burn our necks with light. and we consulted w hermes with the stated purpose of selling our art: i will give my left arm for your right shoulder and sink my teeth into. and hermes understood this and hermes offered us an online store and we sold our chapbooks there.

WHERE SHOULD WE MAKE OUR HOME?

i don't think there is a simple answer but i am sick from being an ungrounded mobile sprout wandering around and not accomplishing much. lackadaisical photosynthesis. the cartoon cars with police lights on top will never stop screaming. i'm told we need them to inspect our dreams and the tallest stalk in my neighbour's garden is topped by a cloud of red blossoms.

Theodore Fox, 24, Edmonton, Alberta, Canada

My name is Theodore Fox, and I'm 24 years old. I am a poet, critic, and performance artist from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada, currently serving as the writer-in-residence for Latitude 53 gallery here in Edmonton.
